# 🗣️ Interview Scripts & Narratives

Your first and last impressions are the most critical. Use these scripts to nail the "Introduction" and "Why us" phases.

---

## 1. Tell Me About Yourself (2-Minute Version)

*   **Part 1: The Hook (20 sec)**: Who you are professionally and your "Superpower."
    *   *"I'm a software engineer with X years of experience, specializing in distributed systems and performance optimization. My superpower is taking legacy monolithic systems and modularizing them into highly available services without downtime."*
*   **Part 2: The Journey (40 sec)**: Key highlights only. Show a growth trajectory.
*   **Part 3: The Peak (30 sec)**: Your most recent/relevant achievement.
*   **Part 4: The Pivot (20 sec)**: Why you're here and what you want next.

---

## 2. The 30-Second Elevator Pitch

Use this when meeting a recruiter or at the start of a quick coffee chat.

*"I'm an engineer at [Current Company] where I recently led the migration of our payment gateway, reducing processing failures by 15%. I'm a deep-dive specialist in Java and distributed transactions, and I'm currently looking to transition into a Senior role where I can lead architectural decisions for high-scale consumer products."*

---

## 3. "Why [Company]?" (The Alignment Script)

**The Trap:** "I want to work here because you're a big successful company."
**The Gold Standard:** **Pull, not Push.**

*"I've been following [Company]'s engineering blog for a while, especially the piece on how you handle global state in [Product]. At [My Company], I faced a similar challenge with [Project], and I'm excited to bring my experience with [Technology] to a team that is solving [Specific Challenge] at this massive scale."*

---

## 4. "Why are you leaving?" (The Narrative)

**The Rule:** Never speak ill of your current company or manager.

*"I've had a great journey at [Company] and learned a lot about [Skill], but I've reached a point where the local technical challenges are becoming repetitive. I'm looking for a more complex domain where I can leverage my expertise in [Skill] and grow my [Leadership/Architecture] responsibilities."*
