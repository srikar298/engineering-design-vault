# 🎯 Mock Prep & Polish Guide

Drafting your stories is only 50% of the work. The other 50% is the **delivery**. This guide provides advanced techniques to polish your performance.

---

## 1. The Mirror Method (Body Language)
Record yourself answering 3 questions. Watch the video twice:
1.  **Without Audio**: Check your eye contact, hand gestures, and nervous ticks (e.g., touching hair, shifting in chair).
2.  **With Audio**: Check your energy levels and filler word frequency.

---

## 2. Filler Word Tracking
Target: **< 3 filler words per story.**
Common fillers: "Um", "Uh", "Like", "Basically", "You know", "Right?".

**The Fix:** **The Strategic Pause.** Instead of saying "Um," just stop talking for 1 second. It makes you look thoughtful and authoritative.

---

## 3. The Depth Test (Follow-up Questions)
Assume the interviewer will ask at least 2 follow-ups per story. Prepare for these common ones:
*   *"What was your specific thought process behind that decision?"*
*   *"What were the alternatives you considered?"*
*   *"If you had more time/resources, what would you have changed?"*
*   *"What was the team's reaction to your proposal?"*
*   *"How did you measure the success of that outcome?"*

---

## 4. The Bridge Method (When you don't have a story)
If they ask a question you haven't prepared for:
1.  **Strategic Pause**: "That's a great question. Let me think for a moment." (3 seconds).
2.  **Bridge**: "I haven't faced that exact scenario, but I dealt with something very similar when..."
3.  **Deploy**: Use your closest story and explicitly state how the principle applies to their question.

---

## 5. Questions to Ask the Interviewer
Always have 3-4 thoughtful questions ready.
*   *"What's the biggest technical challenge the team is facing in the next 6 months?"*
*   *"How does the team balance shipping speed with technical debt and quality?"*
*   *"What does engineering excellence look like on your team?"*
*   *"How are technical disagreements typically resolved here?"*
