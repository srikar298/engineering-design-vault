# 🏢 Company Profile: Amazon

Amazon's interview process is uniquely focused on their **16 Leadership Principles (LPs)**. Almost every question asked in every round will map directly to one or more LPs.

---

## 💎 The 16 Leadership Principles & Key Questions

### 1. Customer Obsession
*   **Signal**: Starting with customer problems and working backwards.
*   **Key Question**: *"Tell me about a time you had to make a technical decision that was unpopular with developers but benefited customers."*

### 2. Ownership
*   **Signal**: End-to-end accountability. Never saying "not my job."
*   **Key Question**: *"Tell me about a time you noticed a major system bug outside your area and resolved it without being asked."*

### 3. Invent & Simplify
*   **Signal**: Finding simple solutions to complex problems. Innovation.
*   **Key Question**: *"Describe a time you simplified a complex system architecture or development process."*

### 4. Are Right, A Lot
*   **Signal**: Strong engineering judgment, seeking diverse viewpoints, proving theories with data.
*   **Key Question**: *"Tell me about a time you made an architectural decision with incomplete information."*

### 5. Learn & Be Curious
*   **Signal**: Constant learning, exploring new technologies.
*   **Key Question**: *"Tell me about a time you learned a new technology or framework for a project under a tight deadline."*

### 6. Hire & Develop the Best
*   **Signal**: Mentoring, raising the talent bar of the team.
*   **Key Question**: *"Tell me about a time you helped another engineer grow or overcome a technical roadblock."*

### 7. Insist on the Highest Standards
*   **Signal**: Refusing to compromise on testing, quality, or documentation.
*   **Key Question**: *"Tell me about a time you pushed back on a deadline because shipping would compromise code quality."*

### 8. Think Big
*   **Signal**: Scaling systems, bold technical directions.
*   **Key Question**: *"Describe a project you led where you designed the system to handle 10x its current volume."*

### 9. Bias for Action
*   **Signal**: Speed and execution. Shicking fast over perfect planning.
*   **Key Question**: *"Tell me about a time you shipped a feature quickly to validate a hypothesis before a project was fully specified."*

### 10. Frugality
*   **Signal**: Resource optimization. Reducing cloud costs, saving developer hours.
*   **Key Question**: *"Tell me about a time you optimized a database or service configuration to reduce server costs."*

### 11. Earn Trust
*   **Signal**: Humility, vocal self-criticism, acknowledging mistakes.
*   **Key Question**: *"Tell me about a time you failed to meet a deadline. How did you communicate and recover?"*

### 12. Dive Deep
*   **Signal**: Connecting to the details, profiling, root-cause diagnostics.
*   **Key Question**: *"Tell me about a complex bug you debugged in production. What was your systematic search process?"*

### 13. Have Backbone; Disagree & Commit
*   **Signal**: Respectfully challenging technical decisions, then committing 100%.
*   **Key Question**: *"Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager's technical direction. How did you present your case?"*

### 14. Deliver Results
*   **Signal**: Overcoming obstacles to ship high-quality products.
*   **Key Question**: *"Describe a time you had to deliver a system under tight timeline constraints with key resource gaps."*

### 15. Strive to be Earth's Best Employer
*   **Signal**: Empathy, psychological safety, growth.
*   **Key Question**: *"How do you handle creating an inclusive environment on a team with differing skill levels?"*

### 16. Success & Scale Bring Responsibility
*   **Signal**: Compliance, privacy, long-term impact.
*   **Key Question**: *"Tell me about a time you designed a system where customer privacy or compliance was a major constraint."*

## 🕵️ The Bar Raiser Round
The **Bar Raiser** is an interviewer from a different department who represents the global talent bar. Their role is to ensure that every hire raises the average talent bar of the team.
*   They focus heavily on LPs and search for inconsistencies in your stories.
*   They evaluate your reaction to follow-up questions.
*   **Pro-Tip**: If the interviewer asks, *"What specifically did YOU do?"* repeatedly, they are testing for your individual contribution (checking if you are taking credit for a team win).

---

## 🎯 Tips for Amazon Behavioral Loops
1.  **Format strictly in STAR+**: Keep Situation/Task under 30 seconds. Focus 60% of the time on your Actions.
2.  **Use "I" not "We"**: Never say, *"We migrated the service."* Say, *"The team migrated, and my specific contribution was designing the partition indexing."*
3.  **Include metrics**: Every story must have a result metric. Amazon is a data-driven company.
